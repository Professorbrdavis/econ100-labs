Interactive Graph Lab 3: AD–AS Shifts (Macro)

Overview

Shift aggregate demand (spending) and short-run aggregate supply (costs/productivity) to see how the short-run equilibrium changes. Use the story box to practice macro explanations in plain language.

Key terms

AD SRAS LRAS price level potential output supply shock

Tip: Move one slider at a time, narrate what changed, then describe whether you see a movement along a curve or a shift of a curve.

Interactive widget

Quick interpretation: AD shifts are “spending stories” (C/I/G/NX). SRAS shifts are often “cost stories” (input prices, productivity, expected inflation).

Controls

Right = higher AD. Left = lower AD.
Right = higher SRAS (lower costs / higher productivity). Left = lower SRAS (negative supply shock).

Self-check

Create a “stagflation” picture: shift SRAS left. What happens to the price level and output in the short run?

Answers and coaching

  • AD right → higher price level and higher output (short run).
  • SRAS left (negative supply shock) → higher price level and lower output (stagflation story).
  • LRAS marks potential output; long-run adjustments move output back toward potential.
