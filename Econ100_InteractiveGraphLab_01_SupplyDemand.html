Interactive Graph Lab 1: Supply and Demand Shifts

Overview

Use the sliders to shift demand and supply, then watch how the equilibrium changes. This is a low-stakes way to practice the “shift vs movement along” skill that shows up constantly in ECON 100.

Key terms

demand shifter supply shifter equilibrium surplus shortage movement along vs shift

Tip: Move one slider at a time, narrate what changed, then describe whether you see a movement along a curve or a shift of a curve.

Common confusion: If you move the price slider, you are changing an axis variable, so you should talk about a movement along a curve. If you move a shifter (income, input costs), you should talk about a shift.

Controls

Left = lower demand. Right = higher demand.
Left = lower supply (higher costs). Right = higher supply (lower costs).
Optional: set a price and see whether the market has a shortage or surplus.

Self-check

Try this: increase demand and describe what happens to equilibrium price and quantity. Then reset and increase supply.

Answers and coaching

How to talk about what you see:

  • “Demand shifts right” → equilibrium price rises and equilibrium quantity rises (in the basic model).
  • “Supply shifts right” → equilibrium price falls and equilibrium quantity rises (in the basic model).
  • If the posted price is below equilibrium → shortage (upward pressure on price).
  • If the posted price is above equilibrium → surplus (downward pressure on price).
