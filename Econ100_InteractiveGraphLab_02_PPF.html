Interactive Graph Lab 2: Production Possibilities Frontier (PPF)

Overview

Choose an output level for Good X and watch the frontier point update. Then shift resources/technology to see the PPF move. Use the opportunity cost readout to connect the picture to the concept.

Key terms

PPF scarcity efficiency feasible vs infeasible opportunity cost economic growth

Tip: Move one slider at a time, narrate what changed, then describe whether you see a movement along a curve or a shift of a curve.

On this page

  • Interactive widget
  • Answers and coaching

Interactive widget

How to interpret the curve: Points on the curve are efficient. Points inside are inefficient. Points outside are not feasible with current resources and technology.

Controls

Right = outward shift (growth). Left = inward shift (loss).

Self-check

Move Good X to the right. Does the opportunity cost (the sacrifice of Good Y) rise as you specialize? Use the readout to support your answer.

Answers and coaching

  • If the curve is bowed outward, opportunity cost typically increases as you produce more of one good.
  • Outward shifts represent growth (more resources or better technology).
  • Trade does not change the production frontier itself, but it can let consumption exceed the PPF through specialization and exchange.
